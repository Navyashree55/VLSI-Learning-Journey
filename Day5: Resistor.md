# Resistor

## Definition
- A resistor is a passive electrical component that limits the flow of electric current.
- it only dissipates energy in the form of heat.

## Unit
- SI Unit: Ohm (Ω)

## Formula
R = V / I

(Relation from Ohm’s Law: V = IR)

---

## Types of Resistors

### 1. Fixed Resistor
- Resistance value is fixed during manufacturing
- Cannot be changed

### 2. Variable Resistor
- Resistance can be adjusted manually
- Example: Potentiometer (using knob or screw)

---

## Common Uses
- Current limiting
- Voltage division
- Pull-up and pull-down resistors in digital circuits

### Tolerance
- Gold  = ±5%
- Silver = ±10%
- No band = ±20%

---

## Band Types

### 3-Band Resistor
- 1st band → Digit
- 2nd band → Digit
- 3rd band → Multiplier
- Default tolerance = ±20%

---

### 4-Band Resistor
- 1st band → Digit
- 2nd band → Digit
- 3rd band → Multiplier
- 4th band → Tolerance

---

### 5-Band Resistor
- 1st, 2nd, 3rd → Digits
- 4th → Multiplier
- 5th → Tolerance

---

### 6-Band Resistor
- 1st, 2nd, 3rd → Digits
- 4th → Multiplier
- 5th → Tolerance
- 6th → Temperature Coefficient (TCR)

---

## Example

Green – Red – Blue – Gold

- Green = 5  
- Red = 2  
- Blue = Multiplier (10⁶)  
- Gold = ±5%

Resistance = 52 × 10⁶ = 52 MΩ

---

## Key Insight
- Higher resistance → lower current
- Resistors are fundamental components used in all electronic circuits
