 

**Overview**

The Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT) is a semiconductor device used for:

- Amplification
-  Switching

“Bipolar” means both electrons and holes participate in current conduction.

---

 **Structure of BJT**

A BJT consists of three regions:

Region| Doping Level| Function

Emitter (E)| Heavily doped| Injects charge carriers

Base (B)| Thin & lightly doped| Controls carrier flow

Collector (C)| Moderately doped| Collects carriers

---

**Types of BJT**

🔹 NPN Transistor

- Majority carriers: Electrons
- Current flow: Collector → Emitter
- Preferred in circuits

🔹 PNP Transistor

- Majority carriers: Holes
- Current flow: Emitter → Collector

 Why NPN is preferred?
Electrons have higher mobility → faster operation and better performance.

---

 **Symbol Representation**

- NPN → Arrow points outward
- PNP → Arrow points inward

---

**Working Principle (NPN)**

- Emitter-Base (EB) Junction → Forward biased (~0.7V)
- Collector-Base (CB) Junction → Reverse biased (~5–10V)

Operation:

1. Electrons are injected from emitter into base
2. Base is thin → very few recombine
3. Most electrons move to collector
4. Small base current controls large collector current

---

 **Depletion Region Formation**

Before applying voltage:

- Charge carriers diffuse across junctions
- After equilibrium, diffusion stops
- No free carriers at junction → depletion region forms

---

 **Behavior with Applied Voltage**

🔹 EB Junction (Forward Bias)

- Depletion region decreases
- Allows electrons to flow into base

🔹 CB Junction (Reverse Bias)

- Depletion region increases
- Strong electric field created
- Electrons are swept into collector

 Key Idea:

- Forward bias → reduces depletion region
- Reverse bias → increases depletion region

---

 **Current Relations**

I_E = I_B + I_C

I_C = \beta \times I_B


Where:

- I_E = Emitter current
- I_B = Base current
- I_C = Collector current
- \beta = Current gain

---

**Base Current (IB)**

- Caused by recombination of electrons with holes in base
- Base is very thin → minimal recombination
- Enables large collector current

---

 **Key Takeaways**

- Base controls the transistor
- Small base current → large collector current
- EB junction injects carriers
- CB junction collects carriers
- Fundamental concept behind amplification
