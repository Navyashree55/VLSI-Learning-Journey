A **Field Effect Transistor (FET)** is a type of transistor that uses an electric field to control the flow of current through a semiconductor channel.

---

## Basic Structure

- Gate (G)  
- Drain (D)  
- Source (S)  

**Current flows from Drain to Source**

---

## Working Principle

- When you apply voltage to the **Gate**, it creates an **electric field**.  
- This electric field controls the flow of current between **Drain and Source**.  
- No direct current flows into the gate → **high input impedance**.

---

## Types of FET

1. **JFET (Junction FET)**  
2. **MOSFET (Most used in VLSI)**  

---

## Characteristics

- Voltage-controlled device  
- Very high input impedance  
- Low power consumption  
- Fast switching speed  
- Used as amplifiers, switches, logic gates, and in memory cells
