# Diode

## Definition
- A diode is a semiconductor device that allows current to flow in **only one direction**.

## Unit
- Forward voltage drop measured in **Volts (V)**

---

## Forward Voltage Drop
- Silicon diode ≈ 0.7 V  
- Germanium diode ≈ 0.3 V

---

## Working
- Consists of a **PN junction**  
- **Forward biased:** Conducts current  
- **Reverse biased:** Blocks current  
- **Zener diode:** Conducts in reverse after breakdown voltage

---

## Behavior

- Controls direction of current flow

### Forward Bias:
- Current flows  
- Acts like a closed switch (after voltage drop)

### Reverse Bias:
- Current blocked  
- Acts like an open switch (until breakdown voltage for Zener diode)

---

## Key Formulas
- Diode current:  
\[
I = I_s (e^{V/V_T} - 1)
\]  
- Power dissipation:  
\[
P = V_f \times I_f
\]  

---

## Applications
- Rectifiers (AC → DC)  
- Voltage regulation (Zener diode)  
- LEDs (Light emission)  
- Signal modulation/demodulation  
- Protection circuits  

---

## Key Insight
- Diode **controls the direction of current**  
- Forward bias conducts, reverse bias blocks
