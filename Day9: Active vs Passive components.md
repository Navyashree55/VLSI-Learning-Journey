# Day 9: Active vs Passive Components

## 1. Passive Components

### Definition
- Passive components are components that **cannot generate or amplify energy**.
- They **do not require external power** to operate.

### Examples
- Resistor  
- Capacitor  
- Inductor  

---

### What they do
- Store energy → (Capacitor, Inductor)  
- Dissipate energy → (Resistor)  
- Do not strongly control the direction of current  

---

## 2. Active Components

### Definition
- Active components are components that **can control current or voltage**.
- They **require external power (bias)** to operate.
- Some active components can also **provide power gain (amplification)**.

### Examples
- Diode  
- Transistor  
- Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp)  
- Integrated Circuits (ICs)  

---

### What they do
- Control current and voltage  
- Act like switches  
- Some can amplify signals (e.g., transistor, op-amp)  

---

## Key Insight

- Passive components → do not amplify, only store or dissipate energy  
- Active components → control the flow of current and may amplify signals
